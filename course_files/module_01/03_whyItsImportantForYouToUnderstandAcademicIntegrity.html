Why It’s Important for You to Understand Academic Integrity

First, we need to understand why it's so important for students to understand academic integrity. Ask yourself the following questions:

  • Why do you have to learn about and understand academic integrity and misconduct?

  • What does it mean with regard to your learning?

Increasing Your Capacity to Get a Job & Gain Respect

Well, one of the most important aspects of academic integrity that you should understand is that our Academic Integrity Policy here at UNF was created not as a way to punish students, but as a way to protect students and their futures after graduating from our university. As a whole, it is important that, as an academic community of learners, we are mindful that we're putting good systems in place to protect students and to protect the quality of the programs we are offering.

Academic integrity isn't just about students; it's about all of us as a UNF community, and these structures are put in place to protect you and to verify the validity of our programs and the capabilities of our graduates.

If UNF graduates don't understand the value of academic integrity, it may lead to a negative perception of the university and its graduates, making it difficult for students to get jobs and be respected in their industry. This is part of UNF's commitment to you and your studies.

How Academic Integrity Protects You

More specifically, an understanding of academic integrity can help to protect you by:

  • Supporting Student Learning,

  • Promoting Validity & Reliability of Research Findings,

  • Promoting Respect for Learning,

  • Preparing Students to Practice Ethical Behaviour in Their Future Careers.

Supporting Student Learning

Academic misconduct compromises the learning environment. Shortcut solutions like plagiarism, cheating, and academic dishonesty deprive students of learning opportunities. Presumably, you're here at UNF to learn and to further your education to improve your opportunities in the future. When submissions are not your own work, you're losing the opportunity to practice the concepts that you have been taught. You're also losing the opportunity to receive accurate feedback from your instructor on the work, so there is no evaluation of learning happening.

When your submissions are not your own, it's impossible for instructors to get an accurate picture of the achievement of learning outcomes. It also limits the instructor's ability to provide constructive feedback on your work, further limiting student development and learning.

When you engage in academic misconduct, whether intentionally or not, you are compromising your learning.

Promoting Validity & Reliability of Research Findings

If information is being provided in an academic context, it should be verifiably true. If you present research information without a citation, your reader has no way of tracing the information you're giving them. This means that there is no identifiable verification that the information you're presenting is actually true, therefore, your research may be deemed invalid.

This is yet another reason that academic integrity and citation are important in academic work.

Promoting Respect for Learning

Academic misconduct shows disrespect for the work and efforts of other academics. It is not fair to fellow researchers and thinkers to claim someone else's ideas or words as your own.

You must always give credit to the original author.

Preparing Students to Practice Ethical Behaviour in Their Future Careers

Upholding academic integrity standards in your educational institution reflects a desire and ability to conduct yourself ethically, both in an academic and professional context.

This is a trait that is highly valued in the workplace, so showing a commitment to academic integrity in your studies is a good indication to your future employer.

In short, academic integrity protects the university's reputation, increasing graduates' capacity to get jobs and gain respect in their industry through:

  • Supporting student learning,

  • Promoting validity and reliability of research findings,

  • Promoting respect for learning,

  • Preparing students to practice ethical behaviour in their future careers.
