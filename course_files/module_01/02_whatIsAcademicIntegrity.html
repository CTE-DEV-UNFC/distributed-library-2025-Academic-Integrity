What Is Academic Integrity?

We'll start by defining what Academic Integrity means. Linked to the idea of Academic Integrity is the term Academic Misconduct. You need to ask yourself the following questions:

  • What is academic misconduct?

  • What are the implications of these terms in your studies here at UNF?

Defining Academic Integrity

The Cambridge Dictionary defines academic integrity as,

"The quality of being honest and having strong moral principles".

Academic Integrity, then, means that all members of the academic community must adhere to principles of honesty and moral ethics when it comes to the production and circulation of academic work.

Academic Misconduct

Academic Misconduct occurs when the standards of academic integrity are not met or lived up to. The University of Niagara Falls Canada defines Academic Misconduct as,

"Any action that is not conducive to the principles of integrity, honesty, respect, fairness and responsibility and that is engaged in to obtain any type of academic advantage or credit".

This definition is inclusive of a multitude of different types of misconduct, including:

  • Cheating,
  • Academic dishonesty,
  • Fabrication,
  • Plagiarism,
  • Re-use of work.

In this module, we’ll discuss what each of these terms means in the context of academic misconduct.
