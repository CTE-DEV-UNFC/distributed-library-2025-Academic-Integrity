Student Responsibilities with Academic Integrity

Now that we have a clearer understanding of academic integrity at UNF, what are some of the things that UNF students are responsible for in an Academic Integrity context?

Student Responsibilities

Students at UNF are responsible for:

  • Ensuring they understand what constitutes academic misconduct,

  • Consulting with instructors and Library staff for advice and clarification,

  • Familiarizing themselves with UNF policies and expectations regarding academic conduct and integrity.

Upon completion of this learning module, you should have a clear understanding of these responsibilities and feel confident in upholding these responsibilities during your time as a UNF student.

Potential Penalties

It is important to understand the potential penalties students may face for allegations of academic misconduct. Based on the information presented in this module, we know that academic misconduct is a serious offence. If you are convicted of academic misconduct here at UNF, there are some serious academic penalties that may be incurred.

Each Academic Incident Triggers the Following

Each academic incident at UNF will trigger the following:

  • First Incident

    Failure of the assignment or test.

  • Second Incident

    Failure of the course.

  • Third Incident

    Expulsion from the University (student will be permitted to reapply to the University after one year has passed).

  • Fourth Incident

    Permanent expulsion from UNF.
