Introduction to Academic Integrity

This module will cover a generalized overview of Academic Integrity.

You will learn what academic misconduct means, how the University of Niagara Falls Canada defines it, and the cultural considerations that come into play in these conversations. Then, we'll discuss the different types of academic misconduct that exist and provide some strategies to avoid academic misconduct allegations.

Finally, we'll go over what your responsibilities are in regard to academic misconduct as a student at UNF and will provide an overview of the penalties you may encounter if you are accused of misconduct.

We'll also quickly remind you of the resources available to help you to ensure that all of your work adheres to the standards of academic integrity at our institution.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to discuss and identify:

  1. How UNF defines Academic Integrity/Misconduct.
  2. Why it is important for students to understand academic integrity.
  3. Cultural considerations connected to Academic Integrity.
  4. Types of Academic Misconduct.
  5. Strategies for avoiding academic misconduct.
  6. Your responsibilities as a student.
  7. Academic penalties for academic misconduct.
  8. Additional resources for students.
