Avoiding Academic Misconduct

Now that we're aware of all of the different types of academic misconduct that exist, we'll talk about some of the ways that you can avoid accidental misconduct in your work.

Proper Referencing & Citation

One way to avoid academic misconduct, and plagiarism in particular, is to ensure you are using proper referencing and citation style.

Here at UNF, currently, all programs are using APA style referencing and citation. Any time you're presenting information that is not your own, you must properly reference it both in-text and in your reference list. Each citation style has specific rules and style guidelines that must be followed. Ensure that you understand the citation guidelines for the assignment you're working on and use a citation style guide to help you to format your references correctly.

Proper Paraphrasing & Summary

Proper paraphrasing and summarizing go hand-in-hand with using proper referencing and citation techniques. Ensure that when you are doing research of any kind, you're compiling and synthesizing information in your own words, or using quotations for sentences and phrases taken directly from the source.

Also be sure to track where information is being taken from in your research. This is important for creating intext citations and for compiling a reference list.

Resources

Citation management tools, like Zotero, exist to help you to track and organize your sources. Instructions for accessing and using Zotero are available on the UNF Library website.

When completing research and compiling information, explain concepts in your own words, and track where information is taken from.

Understand Expectations

Another way to make sure that you're maintaining academic integrity in your work is to ensure that you fully understand the expectations of you in each course you're taking.

  • Listen,

Read

When you start the course, read through the course materials provided to you by your professor, including your course outline and syllabus.

Listen

Listen to your instructors when they discuss course information; it's important that you know what their expectations and requirements are. Each instructor may have different expectations of you and expectations may differ between assignments, so take notes and listen carefully!

Understand

Ensure that you understand which resources are permitted for use in specific assignments and courses, what the expectations are around citations and formatting style guidelines, and whether or not you are permitted to use generative AI tools to assist you in the completion of your work.

If you do not clearly understand these expectations, you put yourself at risk for allegations of academic misconduct, whether it was accidental or not.

Manage Your Time

Another strategy that you can lean on to prevent academic misconduct in your work is proper time management.

Always plan ahead - leave yourself enough time to complete your assignments and prepare for tests.

Students who leave themselves enough time and feel prepared are less likely to engage in academic misconduct because they feel more confident in the work they are able to produce in the time they have allotted. Feelings of insecurity about time and information can lead to a temptation to take shortcuts and resort to academically dishonest behaviour. Don't put yourself in this position!
