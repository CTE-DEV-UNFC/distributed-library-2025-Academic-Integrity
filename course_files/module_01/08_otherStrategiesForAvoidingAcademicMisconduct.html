Other Strategies for Avoiding Academic Misconduct

You’ve learned about some key strategies for avoiding academic misconduct, specifically proper referencing and citation, paraphrasing and summarizing, understanding expectations, and managing your time. Some other strategies you can employ include exercising caution in collaboration and group work, managing academic materials responsibly, asking for clarification, and ensuring you submit your own work always.

Exercise Caution in Collaboration & Group Work

When you are working together with groups of people it is important to exercise caution to ensure that you avoid academic misconduct and remember to always submit your own work.

You should also be careful when sharing your work with fellow classmates and others. Working too closely with a friend or classmate on an assignment that was meant to be completed individually can lead to a violation of academic integrity. Exercising caution in the sharing of information helps to prevent accidental academic misconduct.

While you're encouraged to work with others to further your own understanding of course concepts and idea share, you shouldn't be working together to complete assignments unless you have specifically been instructed to.

Rember, if a classmate or friend has a question, you can try to help, but don't give them the answer.

Don't ever submit work under your name that is not your own. This includes asking someone else to complete your assignments and/or tests for you, asking generative AI tools to complete your work for you, and using websites that offer pre-prepared papers in place of writing a paper yourself. Any work that you hand in for credit must be your own, original submission.

When you're working with a group to complete an assignment, your entire submission should adhere to the academic integrity standards of your institution. This is why it's important to take special precautions when submitting group work.

Often, from an academic integrity perspective, it is safest to complete group work together rather than dividing the assignment into parts and completing them individually, then compiling the parts into one whole document. If you divide the assignment up between group members, it becomes difficult to discern whether or not your group members have used unauthorized sources and/or assistance on their work, putting your whole group at risk for academic misconduct.

If you work collaboratively to complete assignment tasks, you will learn to work with others, will increase your collaborative learning capacity, and will protect yourself from potential academic misconduct.

Manage Academic Materials Responsibly

It is fine to take good notes during classes and save them for your own reference, but you shouldn't be posting this information anywhere or sharing it with anyone else.

An instructor's notes, assignments, and tests are their intellectual property. If you are sharing these materials or posting them online, you're putting yourself at risk for committing theft of intellectual property.

Similarly, if you're accessing things like test banks and assignment solutions, you're accessing confidential academic material without authorization. Both cases constitute academic misconduct and could result in serious consequences.

Ask for Clarification

Finally, if you find yourself unsure about assignment guidelines, resources that you're authorized to use, or whether or not you're allowed to collaborate with other students on a particular task, don't be afraid to ask your instructor for clarification. It is always better to ask for help than to risk academic misconduct, whether it is intentional or not. Your instructors are there to support you in your learning and can provide guidance on assignment expectations and course work. If you aren't sure, ask!

You also have access to both Library and Writing Centre assistance here at UNF, free of charge. If you need help with research or with APA style citation and referencing, you can book a consultation with a Librarian or a Writing Advisor to assist you. Library staff are happy to advise on avoiding plagiarism and maintaining academic integrity in your research and writing tasks.

Remember

Ensure that you are using authorized resources for the courses you're enrolled in and make use of the resources available to you if you need them.

In short, to manage materials responsibly:

  • Save your notes and course materials for your own reference, but don't post them in public places,

  • Don't use posted test banks to study unless it was given to you by your instructor,

  • If you aren't sure about something, ask your instructor for clarification,

  • Visit the UNF Library or the Writing Centre if you have questions about research, referencing, and citation styles.
